Top 15 Authors

  1. Oscar Wilde - Irish poet and playwright known for his witty epigrams and dramas.
  2. William Shakespeare - English playwright and poet, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language.
  3. Mark Twain - American writer and humorist best known for his novels about Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn.
  4. Friedrich Nietzsche - German philosopher who wrote critically on religion, morality, culture, and philosophy.
  5. Albert Einstein - Theoretical physicist renowned for developing the theory of relativity, contributing significantly to the advancement of modern physics.
  6. Ralph Waldo Emerson - American essayist, lecturer, philosopher, and poet who led the transcendentalist movement of the mid-19th century.
  7. Johann Wolfgang von Goethe - German writer and statesman whose works include poetry, dramas, and literature.
  8. George Bernard Shaw - Irish playwright, critic, and polemicist known for his influence on Western theatre, culture, and politics.
  9. Winston Churchill - British statesman who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom and is noted for his leadership during World War II.
  10. Benjamin Franklin - One of the Founding Fathers of the United States, known for his contributions to science, politics, and philosophy.
  11. Abraham Lincoln - 16th President of the United States who led the nation through its Civil War and abolished slavery.
  12. Mahatma Gandhi - Leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule, employing nonviolent civil disobedience.
  13. Voltaire - French Enlightenment writer, historian, and philosopher famous for his wit and advocacy for freedom of speech and religion.
  14. Buddha - Spiritual leader and founder of Buddhism in ancient India, known for his teachings on suffering, enlightenment, and rebirth.
  15. Thomas Jefferson - Third President of the United States, principal author of the Declaration of Independence, and advocate for democracy and individual rights.
